# Downloaded Papers

## Core Alignment Theory

1. **Defining and Characterizing Reward Hacking** (2209.13085_defining_characterizing_reward_hacking.pdf)
   - Authors: Joar Skalse, Nikolaus Howe, Dmitrii Krasheninnikov, David Krueger
   - Year: 2022 (NeurIPS)
   - arXiv: 2209.13085
   - Why relevant: First formal definition of reward hacking and hackability. Key impossibility theorem for unhackability over continuous policy sets. Foundational for defining τ.

2. **Reward Hacking as Equilibrium under Finite Evaluation** (2603.28063_reward_hacking_equilibrium_finite_eval.pdf)
   - Authors: Jiacheng Wang, Jinbin Huang
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2603.28063
   - Why relevant: Proves distortion inevitability under finite evaluation. Introduces distortion index and Goodhart-Campbell transition conjecture — directly related to misalignment threshold.

3. **Unified Alignment Between Agents, Humans, and Environment** (2402.07744_unified_alignment_agents_humans.pdf)
   - Authors: Zonghan Yang, An Liu, Zijun Liu et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2402.07744
   - Why relevant: Framework for multi-dimensional alignment of autonomous agents.

4. **Specification Gaming in Reasoning Models** (2502.13295_specification_gaming_reasoning_models.pdf)
   - Authors: Alexander Bondarenko et al.
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2502.13295
   - Why relevant: Demonstrates specification gaming in modern reasoning models — empirical evidence for reward hacking in advanced LLMs.

## Adversarial Robustness and Scaling Laws

5. **Scaling Compute Is Not All You Need for Adversarial Robustness** (2312.13131_scaling_compute_adversarial_robustness.pdf)
   - Authors: Edoardo Debenedetti, Zishen Wan, Maksym Andriushchenko et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - arXiv: 2312.13131
   - Why relevant: Derives scaling laws for adversarial robustness. Power law: accuracy = C × FLOPs^α with α ≈ 0.01.

6. **Adversarial Robustness Limits via Scaling-Law and Human-Alignment Studies** (2404.09349_adversarial_robustness_scaling_laws.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2404.09349
   - Why relevant: First scaling laws for adversarial training relating model size, data, and robustness.

7. **Scaling Trends in Language Model Robustness** (2407.18213_scaling_trends_LLM_robustness.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2407.18213
   - Why relevant: Shows larger models benefit more from adversarial training but scaling alone insufficient.

8. **Scaling Patterns in Adversarial Alignment** (2511.13788_scaling_patterns_adversarial_alignment.pdf)
   - Authors: Samuel Nathanson, Cynthia Matuszek, Rebecca Williams
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2511.13788
   - Why relevant: Log-linear scaling of adversarial harm with model size ratio. Directly supports power-law hypothesis.

9. **Latent Adversarial Training Improves Robustness** (2407.15549_latent_adversarial_training_robustness.pdf)
   - Authors: Various
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2407.15549
   - Why relevant: Robustness improvement techniques for LLMs.

10. **Assessing Adversarial Robustness of LLMs** (2405.02764_assessing_adversarial_robustness_LLMs.pdf)
    - Authors: Various
    - Year: 2024
    - arXiv: 2405.02764
    - Why relevant: Empirical assessment of LLM robustness with scaling analysis.
